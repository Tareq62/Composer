Tareq Abuissa is a New York-based composer, lyricist, and music director. His four stage musicals include the tech industry satire South of Market: The Musical (2016, 2017), while his scores for the screen include three USC Animation thesis films (2014) and two video interview series with Lincoln Center (2013). Abuissa is the songwriter for the psych-folk band Ruby Jubilee's eponymous debut (2018), while his chamber music has been peformed in Columbia's Miller Theatre.

A lifelong cellist and multi-instrumentalist, Abuissa has performed cello Off-Broadway for Marvell Rep's The Dybbuk (2011), as well as bass guitar in South of Market's band. While studying Music and Mathematics at Columbia University, he co-composed the 118th Varsity Show, The Corporate Core (2012), and his composition teachers included Fred Lerdahl and Osvaldo Golijov. Abuissa is a member of the Society of Composers & Lyricists.
