Tareq Abuissa is a New York-based Composer-Lyricist and Music Director. His four stage musicals include the tech industry satire South of Market: The Musical (2016, 2017), and his scores for the screen include two video interview series with Lincoln Center (2013), three Animation thesis films at the University of Southern California (2014), and a Dance thesis at Barnard College (2014). He wrote the music and lyrics for the psych-folk debut album Ruby Jubilee (2018), influenced by his background composing chamber music, which has been peformed in Columbia's Miller Theatre.

A lifelong cellist and multi-instrumentalist, Abuissa has performed Off-Broadway as the cellist for Marvell Rep's The Dybbuk (2011) and as the bass guitarist for South of Market: The Musical's band. While studying Music and Mathematics at Columbia University, he co-composed the 118th Varsity Show, The Corporate Core (2012), and his composition teachers included Fred Lerdahl and Osvaldo Golijov. Abuissa is a member of the Society of Composers & Lyricists.
